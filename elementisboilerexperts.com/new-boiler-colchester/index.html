Types of new gas boiler

Combi boilers
A combi boiler, also known as a combination boiler, is a popular choice for small to medium-sized homes. It is a compact and energy-efficient central heating system that combines a water heater and boiler in one unit. It provides hot water on demand without the need for a separate water tank which saves space and reduces fitting costs. 
System boilers
A system boiler works by heating water stored in a cylinder or tank, which is then distributed to radiators and hot water outlets throughout a property. It is typically placed in medium to larger homes where there is a higher demand for hot water and heating, and is more suitable for homes with multiple bathrooms. 
Heat only boilers
A heat-only boiler, also known as a regular or conventional boiler, requires a separate hot water cylinder and cold-water storage tank. This type of boiler is typically placed in larger homes with high hot water demands and where there are multiple bathrooms. It is also commonly used in older properties where the existing heating system requires replacement.

How much does a new boiler cost?

The cost of a new boiler in Colchester varies based on several factors. Firstly, the type of boiler you choose impacts the price. Combi boilers tend to be more budget-friendly, while system or conventional boilers may cost more upfront but offer advantages in larger homes.

Additionally, the brand and model of the boiler influence the cost. Renowned brands may have a higher initial price, but they often come with a reputation for reliability. Installation complexity, local labour rates, and required components like thermostats can also affect costs.

Consider energy efficiency when evaluating expenses. High-efficiency models might have a greater upfront cost but can lead to significant long-term savings on energy bills.

To obtain a precise estimate for your new boiler installation, please book a survey with our skilled team. We'll assess your specific heating requirements, property layout, and any unique factors to provide you with a tailored no-obligation quote.

Is it worth getting a new boiler now?

If you are hating problems with your heating, investing in a new boiler can be a worthwhile decision. The right timing depends on various factors. We would recommend that you consider the following:

  1. Age and Efficiency: If your current boiler is over 10 years old and less energy-efficient, a replacement can lead to significant cost savings on your energy bills. Modern boilers are designed to be more efficient, reducing both your carbon footprint and expenses.
  2. Reliability: Frequent breakdowns and repairs can be a sign that your boiler is reaching the end of its lifespan. Investing in a new boiler can provide peace of mind and eliminate the inconvenience of sudden heating failures.
  3. Energy Savings: New boilers come with improved energy efficiency ratings. Over time, the reduced energy consumption can offset the initial investment, making it a financially sensible choice.
  4. Government Incentives: Keep an eye on government incentives and grants for energy-efficient home improvements. These can help reduce the cost.
  5. Safety and Environmental Concerns: Older boilers may pose safety risks and emit higher levels of carbon emissions. Upgrading to a modern, eco-friendly model can enhance safety and reduce your environmental impact.
  6. Comfort: New boilers often provide better heating control and consistent warmth throughout your home, enhancing your overall comfort.

If you aren't sure whether it's the right time for a new boiler, consult with our experts. We can assess your current system, heating needs, available options and costs to help you make an informed decision on wether or repair or replace.
