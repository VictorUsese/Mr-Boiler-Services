]]> How to save money on your central heating bills https://elementisboilerexperts.com/advice/how-to-save-money-on-your-central-heating-bills/ Mon, 23 Oct 2023 06:15:56 +0000 https://elementisboilerexperts.com/?p=1312 If you're like most people, you're always looking for ways to save money. Your energy bills are no exception. There are a number of simple things you can do to cut down on your energy usage and lower your bills. Read on for some tips.

 

As the temperature outside begins to drop, so do our energy bills. This is especially true in the United Kingdom, where heating costs can be quite high. There are some simple ways, however, that you can save money on your heating bills without making any major changes or sacrifices. By following a few of these tips, you can keep your home warm and comfortable all winter long without breaking the bank.

Save money and stay comfortable by programming your boiler.

If you have a boiler in your home, did you know that you can programme it to operate at different times of the day? This can be really useful in maximising comfort and saving money on your energy bills.  For example, if you know you're going to be out of the house during the day, you can program your boiler to only operate while you're home. This way, you're not heating an empty house and wasting energy.

Turn down the thermostat on your boiler.

You might not realise  it, but your boiler uses a lot of energy. In fact, it's one of the biggest energy users in your home. Turning down the thermostat can save you money on your energy bills and help to prolong the life of your boiler.

Use a smart meter to more easily identify where you're using the most energy.

If you're looking to cut down on your energy usage, a smart meter can be a helpful tool. By tracking your energy consumption, you can identify areas where you could make changes to save money. In this Article post, we'll explore how to use a smart meter to help understand where you're using the most energy. We'll also provide some tips on how to reduce your overall energy consumption. 

Invest in energy-efficient appliances.

One of the best ways to reduce your energy usage is to invest in energy-efficient appliances. This is because they use less energy to operate, which can save you money on your energy bills. In this Article post, we'll take a look at some of the most energy-efficient appliances available on the market today. We'll also provide some tips on how to choose the right appliance for your home and how to use them efficiently.

Use thermostat valves on your radiators to control the temperature in each room.

If you're looking for a way to control the temperature in each room of your home individually, thermostatic radiator valves (TRVs) are a great option. TRVs can be installed on your existing radiators, and they work by regulating the flow of hot water through the radiator based on the current room temperature. This means that you can set each room to the temperature that you want, without having to worry about overheating or wasting energy. Plus, TRVs can help improve your home's overall energy efficiency. Here's everything you need to know about using TRVs to control the temperature in your home.

Make sure you have Insulation to ensure that no energy is lost.

According to the Energy Saving Trust, insulating your water tank, pipes and radiators could save you around £25 a year on your energy bills. This is because heat is lost through uninsulated surfaces, which means that your boiler has to work harder to maintain the same temperature. In addition, insulating your pipes can help to prevent them from freezing in cold weather.

 

Insulating your water tank is also a good idea if it is located in an unheated area, such as an attic or garage. In order to insulate your water tank, you will need to purchase a water tank jacket. These are available from most hardware stores. For radiators, you can either purchase radiator covers or lengths of foam insulation. Make sure to measure the width and height of your radiator before purchasing covers. 

 

Finally, for pipes, you can use lengths of lagging pipe insulation. Simply cut the lagging to size and then fit it around the pipe. Lagging can be held in place with tape or wire ties. Insulating your water tank, pipes and radiators is a simple and effective way to reduce your energy consumption and save money on your energy bills.

Keeping your boiler on, even if you're away

Even when you're away from home, it's important to keep your boiler on. While it may seem like a waste of energy, there are actually several benefits to doing so. First of all, keeping the boiler on helps to prevent freezing pipes. If the temperature in your home drops too low, the water in your pipes can start to freeze, which can lead to costly repairs. Additionally, keeping the boiler on helps to keep your home warm and dry, which can deter pests from taking up residence. Finally, maintaining a consistent temperature in your home can help to prolong the lifespan of your boiler. So even when you're not at home, it's important to keep the boiler on - it could save you time and money in the long run.

Make sure you have the right boiler for your house.

With so many options available, it's important to choose the right boiler for your home. The type of boiler you select will affect your running costs, so it's important to compare different models before making a decision. You should also consider the size of your home and the number of people who live there. A smaller home with only one or two occupants will require a different boiler than a large home with multiple occupants. By selecting the right boiler for your home, you can save money on your energy bills and enjoy reliable heat and hot water.

 

Conclusion

So, what can you do to save money on your heating this winter? Here are a few simple tips that should help you reduce your energy bills without too much effort. 

 

Keep your home well insulated, make sure your windows and doors are properly sealed, and use draft excluders around the edges of your doors. You can also save money by turning down the thermostat a degree or two – most people don’t need their homes to be as warm as they think they do. 

 

And finally, try to cook meals using the oven or stove instead of the microwave whenever possible – microwaves use more energy than conventional ovens. Follow these tips and you should see a noticeable difference in your next energy bill.

]]> Should i keep my old boiler? https://elementisboilerexperts.com/advice/should-i-keep-my-old-boiler/ Fri, 16 Jun 2023 21:06:05 +0000 https://elementisboilerexperts.com/?p=1088 Deciding whether to keep or replace an old gas boiler depends on several factors. Here are some reasons you might consider keeping your old gas boiler:

Boiler Cost:

If your current boiler is still functioning efficiently and doesn't require frequent repairs, it may be more cost-effective to continue using it rather than investing in a new one. New boilers can be expensive, and the upfront cost might not be justifiable if your current system is working well.

Compatibility:

Replacing a boiler often requires modifications to the heating system, such as changes to pipes, radiators, or controls. If your current system is compatible with modern boilers and meets your heating needs adequately, there may be no urgent need to upgrade.

Environmental impact:

While newer boilers tend to be more energy-efficient and eco-friendly, replacing a working boiler contributes to the carbon footprint associated with manufacturing and disposing of the old unit. If your old boiler is still relatively efficient and not causing excessive emissions, keeping it for now might be a greener choice.

Limited boiler alternatives:

Depending on your location, there might be limited options for alternative heating systems or fuel sources. If gas is readily available and cost-effective in your area, sticking with a gas boiler could be a practical choice.

However, it's important to consider some potential drawbacks of keeping an old gas boiler:

Energy efficiency:

Older boilers may have lower energy efficiency ratings compared to newer models. Upgrading to a high-efficiency boiler could lead to reduced energy consumption and lower utility bills over time.

Reliability and safety: As boilers age, their reliability can decrease, leading to more frequent breakdowns and potential safety hazards. Older boilers might also lack modern safety features present in newer models.

Maintenance and repair costs:

While your current boiler might be working fine now, older units are more likely to require repairs and maintenance in the future. Consider the potential costs of ongoing servicing and repairs when evaluating the long-term viability of your old boiler.

Ultimately, the decision to keep or replace your gas boiler should be based on a thorough evaluation of its efficiency, reliability, safety, and long-term costs. ]]> 6 Undeniable Benefits of a New Boiler for Your Home https://elementisboilerexperts.com/advice/6-undeniable-benefits-of-a-new-boiler-for-your-home/ Wed, 17 May 2023 20:14:00 +0000 https://elementisboilerexperts.com/?p=952 Did you know that over half of what you spend on energy bills goes to heating and hot water? That shows just how important a good, working boiler is for your home and your pocket.

But old boilers aren’t just inefficient. Over time, the constant cycle of breakdowns and repairs can become tiresome and costly. Surely there’s a better way? This post will outline six benefits of a new boiler over yet another repair job.

1. Energy efficient

Many boilers aren’t as efficient as they used to be and waste around 25% of their heat. The older they get, the more likely leaks or faults will develop. You could end up on a constant loop of boiler repairs. With rising energy prices, that could mean losing hundreds of pounds unnecessarily.

New boilers have an A-rating for energy, which means they are at least 90% efficient. They use the more of the heat generated to do what they’re supposed to, making them more economical to run.

2. Cost-effective

When it comes to new boilers, most people focus on the outgoing investment. But don’t forget the savings you’ll make over time. According to the Energy Saving Trust, you can save up to £840 per year by replacing an old boiler with an A-rated model, depending on the rating of the existing boiler and the type of house it’s heating.

3. Precise heating control

Room thermostats are standard on every new boiler, which gives you control over the temperature in each room of the house. So, there is no need to waste energy by heating empty rooms. This provides two new boiler benefits, as it will help lower monthly costs and keep everyone more comfortable.

4. Ongoing service plans

Many people wait until their boiler needs repairs before they contact an engineer. But a huge new boiler benefit is that you don’t have to worry about costs or the hassle of sourcing a professional. Many companies offer an ongoing service plan that gives you annual checks on your heating system by a certified engineer. So, you have peace of mind that you aren’t likely to be hit with huge repair bills, as well as knowing your boiler is safe for use.

5. Peace and quiet

Gone are the days where your boiler is grumbling loudly from its storage cupboard. A new modern boiler won’t be noisy due to years of use, so it won’t keep you awake at night or gurgle in the background when you have visitors.

6. Choose which suits your lifestyle

A major benefit of a new boiler is that you can choose which type will work for you. The three main types of boilers are:

  • Combi boilers – Systems that combine a water heater and boiler together. Providing hot water on demand, they are popular due to their compact size – perfect for smaller homes with limited space.
  • System boilers – A tank or cylinder that heats the water before sending it out to radiators and taps in the home. Bigger homes with more than one bathroom prefer a system boiler as it caters for a higher demand of heating and hot water.
  • Heat only boilers – Also called a ‘conventional’ boiler, it is used in bigger homes or older properties that need their system replaced. They use a separate hot water cylinder and a cold-water storage tank.

]]> What is a boiler warranty? https://elementisboilerexperts.com/advice/what-is-a-boiler-warranty/ Sun, 30 Apr 2023 05:56:37 +0000 https://elementisboilerexperts.com/?p=931

If you're thinking of purchasing a new boiler, one of the things you'll want to consider is the warranty. A boiler warranty is a guarantee from the manufacturer that their product is of good quality and will function as intended. But what does a boiler warranty cover, and how long does it last?

How long does a boiler warranty last?

Most boilers come with a warranty that lasts between 2-12 years, depending on the manufacturer and the type of boiler. This warranty covers the cost of any repairs that need to be made to the boiler within the warranty period, as long as the issue is not caused by user error or lack of maintenance.

The exact details of what is covered by a boiler warranty can vary depending on the manufacturer, so it's important to read the terms and conditions carefully. However, most boiler warranties cover the following:

Here is what is included in your boiler warranty;

Parts and labour

The cost of any replacement parts and the labour required to install them is usually covered by the warranty.

Faults and defects

If the boiler develops a fault or defect within the warranty period, the cost of repairs or replacement is typically covered.

Heat Exchanger

The heat exchanger is the part of the boiler that heats the water, and it is often covered under the warranty for a specific period of time.

Guarantee of workmanship

Some warranties also include a guarantee of workmanship, meaning that if the installation is not done correctly, the manufacturer will cover the cost of any necessary repairs.

Extended warranty

Some manufacturers offer an extended warranty for an additional fee, which can provide additional coverage beyond the standard warranty period.

It's important to note that a boiler warranty will not cover damage caused by user error or lack of maintenance. For example, if the boiler breaks down due to a build-up of limescale, this may not be covered by the warranty if the boiler has not been properly maintained.

In addition, many manufacturers require that the boiler is installed and serviced by a qualified heating engineer in order to maintain the warranty. Failure to do so could result in the warranty being voided.

Conclusion

In conclusion, a boiler warranty is an important consideration when purchasing a new boiler. It can provide peace of mind that your boiler is covered in the event of any issues, and can help to reduce the cost of any necessary repairs. However, it's important to read the terms and conditions carefully and ensure that you properly maintain your boiler in order to maintain the warranty.
